Every year, New York Times developers hosts the TimesOpen series, five events that aim to bring together the NYC developer community for technical talks on a range of topics. We've always been lucky to have great speakers, insightful discussions, and a lot of live coding. This year, for our fourth event, we're trying something new: an open source science fair and symposium.

To kick off the night, our keynote speakers (so far, Jeremy Ashkenas of The New York Times and Zach Holman of GitHub) will talk about how to launch, scale and contribute to open source projects.

We won't have any model volcanoes, potato clocks, or flatworms -- but we will have booths, lightning talks, and pull requests. And ribbons and prizes!
